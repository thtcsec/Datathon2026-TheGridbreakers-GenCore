\begin{abstract}
We present a zero-leakage forecasting pipeline for predicting daily revenue and cost of goods sold (COGS) over a 548-day horizon for a Vietnamese fashion e-commerce platform. Our approach combines Seasonal Naive baselines with a Prophet + LightGBM residual correction ensemble, calibrated for Vietnamese Lunar New Year (T\'\ecircumflex{}t) dynamics. On a 548-day holdout matching the Kaggle test horizon, we achieve MAE of 752k VND (Revenue) and 659k VND (COGS), with R\textsuperscript{2} $\approx$ 0.55. We provide prescriptive business insights from exploratory analysis of 14 datasets spanning 10 years of operations.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction \& Business Context}

This report addresses the Datathon 2026 challenge: forecasting daily revenue for a Vietnamese fashion e-commerce company from January 1, 2023 to July 1, 2024 (548 days), using historical data from July 2012 to December 2022.

The business context demands accurate daily forecasts to optimize inventory allocation, plan promotional campaigns, and manage logistics across Vietnam. The dataset comprises 14 CSV files organized into four layers: Master (products, customers, promotions, geography), Transaction (orders, order items, payments, shipments, returns, reviews), Analytical (daily sales), and Operational (monthly inventory, daily web traffic).

\textbf{Key challenge:} The 548-day forecast horizon creates a severe distribution shift problem. Standard ML models trained on contemporaneous features (web traffic, daily orders) achieve excellent cross-validation scores but catastrophically fail on the actual test set because these features are unavailable during the forecast period.

\section{Exploratory Data Analysis \& Prescriptive Insights}

\subsection{Revenue Leakage Analysis}

Our EDA focused on answering: \textit{where is realized revenue being eroded, and which interventions should be prioritized?} We identified three primary leakage drivers:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Cancellation friction:} 12.5\% of orders are cancelled, with credit card payments showing the highest cancellation rate. Recommendation: implement order confirmation SMS within 30 minutes of placement.
  \item \textbf{Wrong-size returns:} ``wrong\_size'' is the dominant return reason in Streetwear category. Recommendation: deploy a size recommendation engine using purchase history.
  \item \textbf{Promotion erosion:} 39\% of order items use promotions, but discount depth does not proportionally increase order volume. Recommendation: cap discount depth at 25\% and shift budget to targeted campaigns.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Temporal Patterns}

Revenue exhibits strong seasonality driven by:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Weekly cycle:} Weekdays consistently outperform weekends by 15--20\%.
  \item \textbf{T\'\ecircumflex{}t Nguy\^en \DJ\'an:} Revenue surges 3--4 weeks before Lunar New Year, then drops sharply during the holiday week as logistics shut down.
  \item \textbf{Mega-sales:} Double-digit days (9.9, 10.10, 11.11, 12.12) and national holidays (30/4--1/5) create 2--3$\times$ revenue spikes.
  \item \textbf{End-of-month pattern:} Revenue peaks around the last 3--5 days of each month, likely driven by salary cycles.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Data Quality}

All 14 tables were audited for completeness, referential integrity, and outliers. Key findings: zero missing values in the sales target; 80\% missing in \texttt{applicable\_category} (promotions); 12.5\% of orders lack shipment records (cancelled orders). Foreign key integrity is preserved across all joins.

\section{Forecasting Methodology}

\subsection{The Feature Importance Trap}

Our initial approach used XGBoost/LightGBM with contemporaneous features (web traffic, daily orders, inventory levels). This achieved CV MAE of 417k VND but Kaggle MAE of 2M VND---a 5$\times$ degradation. The root cause: tree models learned to map concurrent states rather than temporal dependencies. When these features are unavailable during the 548-day forecast, recursive substitution causes exponential error accumulation.

\subsection{Zero-Leakage Architecture}

We redesigned the pipeline with a strict constraint: \textit{every feature must be deterministically computable for any future date without access to future data}. The architecture has three tiers:

\textbf{Tier 1: Seasonal Naive Backbone (60\% weight).} A blend of (a) 364-day lookback naive (preserving day-of-week alignment), and (b) multi-year DOY/DOW median, adjusted by year-over-year growth rate. This provides a stable, zero-leakage baseline.

\textbf{Tier 2: Prophet + LightGBM Residual Correction (40\% weight).} Prophet (logistic growth, multiplicative seasonality, custom T\'\ecircumflex{}t holidays) generates trend and seasonal components. LightGBM corrects Prophet's residuals using only deterministic features: Fourier terms, calendar indicators, T\'\ecircumflex{}t proximity, mega-sale flags, historical month/DOW profiles, and auxiliary data aggregated as static profiles. Residual corrections are damped exponentially ($e^{-h/400}$) at far horizons to prevent tree-model extrapolation artifacts.

\textbf{Tier 3: T\'\ecircumflex{}t Calibration.} Empirical multipliers computed from 13 years of T\'\ecircumflex{}t-relative revenue profiles (day $-30$ to $+20$ relative to T\'\ecircumflex{}t), applied with optimized strength (Revenue: 0.05, COGS: 0.10).

\subsection{Feature Engineering}

All features are zero-leakage verified:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{Calendar:} month, DOW, DOY, quarter, weekend, month-start/end flags
  \item \textbf{Fourier:} $\sin/\cos$ harmonics for yearly (4 orders) and weekly (2 orders) cycles
  \item \textbf{T\'\ecircumflex{}t:} days-to-T\'\ecircumflex{}t, days-since-T\'\ecircumflex{}t, exponential proximity, pre/post window flags
  \item \textbf{Mega-sales:} binary flags with $\pm$2 day windows for 9.9, 10.10, 11.11, 12.12, 30/4--1/5
  \item \textbf{Historical profiles:} month$\times$DOW median revenue, DOY median, rolling tail statistics
  \item \textbf{Auxiliary:} web traffic and order count medians by month/DOW (static aggregations)
  \item \textbf{Trend:} linear regression projection from historical data
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Validation Strategy}

We use a purged 548-day holdout (2021-07-02 to 2022-12-31) matching the exact Kaggle test horizon length. This ensures our CV score is directly comparable to leaderboard performance, eliminating the CV--LB gap that plagued our initial approach.

The optimal blend is Naive 60\% + Corrected 40\% for both targets, with mild T\'\ecircumflex{}t calibration (strength 0.05--0.10). Prophet's direct weight optimized to near zero, confirming that its primary value is as a feature generator for the LightGBM residual corrector rather than as a standalone forecaster.

\subsection{Feature Importance (SHAP Analysis)}

SHAP analysis of the LightGBM residual corrector reveals the top drivers of forecast corrections:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Historical month median} (\texttt{hist\_m\_med}): Captures seasonal revenue level differences across months.
  \item \textbf{T\'\ecircumflex{}t effect} (\texttt{tet\_effect}): Exponential proximity to Lunar New Year drives the largest individual corrections.
  \item \textbf{1-year lag} (\texttt{lag\_1yr}): Same-period-last-year revenue provides strong signal for residual correction.
  \item \textbf{Day-of-week median} (\texttt{hist\_d\_med}): Weekday vs.\ weekend revenue differential.
  \item \textbf{Fourier terms} (\texttt{sin\_y1}, \texttt{cos\_y1}): Annual cyclical patterns captured by first-order harmonics.
\end{enumerate}

Full SHAP summary and bar plots are provided in the GitHub repository (\texttt{output/shap\_*.png}).

\subsection{Error Analysis}

Error is concentrated in two segments: (1) \textbf{Mega-sale spikes} (30/4--1/5, end-of-month), where the model underestimates peak amplitude; (2) \textbf{Q2--Q3 normal period}, where the model slightly over-predicts due to structural differences between 2021--2022 validation and 2023--2024 test dynamics. Distribution shift between holdout and test remains the primary risk factor.

\section{Conclusion \& Recommendations}

We demonstrated that for long-horizon e-commerce forecasting, simple seasonal baselines outperform complex ML models when contemporaneous features are unavailable. Our zero-leakage architecture achieves stable 548-day forecasts by combining the robustness of Seasonal Naive with the pattern-correction capability of gradient boosting.

\textbf{Business recommendations:}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item Implement size recommendation to reduce wrong-size returns (est.\ 8\% revenue recovery).
  \item Cap promotion discount depth at 25\% and reallocate budget to retention campaigns.
  \item Pre-position inventory 4 weeks before T\'\ecircumflex{}t based on the empirical demand surge profile.
  \item Deploy order confirmation within 30 minutes to reduce cancellation rate.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Reproducibility:} All code is available at \url{https://github.com/thtcsec/Datathon2026-TheGridbreakers-GenCore}. Random seed is fixed at 42. The full pipeline runs in $\sim$90 seconds on a standard laptop.
